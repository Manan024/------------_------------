A.P.J. ABDUL KALAM


Indian aerospace scientist and politician who served as the 11th President of India.

BIOGRAPHY

A.P.J. Abdul Kalam was born as Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam on 15 October, 1931 in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu. He was the youngest of four brothers and one sister in his family. His father, Jainulabdeen, was a boat owner and imam of a local mosque; his mother, Ashiamma, was a housewife. He graduated in Physics from St. Joseph's College, Tiruchirappalli, and later studied aerospace engineering at the Madras Institute of Technology.

SCIENTIFIC CAREER

Early Career and Contributions

Dr. Kalam joined the Aeronautical Development Establishment of the Defence Research and Development Organisation (DRDO) as a scientist after graduating from the Madras Institute of Technology. He was also part of the Indian National Committee for Space Research (INCOSPAR), working under Vikram Sarabhai, the renowned space scientist.

ISRO and SLV-III

Dr. Kalam moved to the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) in 1969, where he was the project director of India's first Satellite Launch Vehicle (SLV-III) which successfully deployed the Rohini satellite in near-earth orbit in July 1980. This achievement made India an exclusive member of the Space Club.

Missile Development Programme

In the 1980s, Kalam headed the Integrated Guided Missile Development Programme (IGMDP) which developed a series of successful missiles including Agni, an intermediate-range ballistic missile, and Prithvi, a tactical surface-to-surface missile. His leadership and vision were crucial in establishing India as a major power in missile technology.

Pokhran-II Nuclear Tests

In 1998, Dr. Kalam played a pivotal role in the Pokhran-II nuclear tests, which established India as a nuclear weapons state. This act demonstrated India's scientific prowess and strategic capability, earning Dr. Kalam the title of "Missile Man of India."

Later Works and Education Advocacy

After his tenure at DRDO and ISRO, Dr. Kalam turned his focus towards educational and societal transformation. He authored several books, the most famous being "Wings of Fire," an autobiography that continues to inspire millions of young Indians. He also initiated the PURA (Providing Urban Amenities in Rural Areas) mission to improve living standards in rural India.

Literary Legacy

Dr. Kalam was not just a scientist but also an inspirational author and educator. His books, speeches, and interactions with students across the country left a profound impact, fostering a spirit of innovation, leadership, and patriotism among the youth.

Influence on Future Generations

Dr. Kalam's life and work continue to inspire countless students, scientists, and ordinary citizens. His vision for a developed India, encapsulated in his "Vision 2020" plan, remains a guiding light for national development initiatives.

Selected Books

  • "Wings of Fire" - An autobiography that chronicles Dr. Kalam's rise from humble beginnings to becoming a key figure in Indian aerospace and nuclear technology.
  • "Ignited Minds" - A call to India's youth to realize their potential and contribute to the nation's progress.
  • "India 2020" - A visionary blueprint for transforming India into a developed nation by 2020.
  • "The Luminous Sparks" - A collection of poems and reflections.
  • "Turning Points" - A sequel to "Wings of Fire," detailing his journey post-2002, including his presidency.

Dr. Kalam's scientific and literary contributions remain a testament to his unwavering commitment to India's progress and his belief in the power of education and innovation.

POLITICAL CAREER

Dr. Kalam served as the 11th President of India from 2002 to 2007, known as the "People's President" for his humility, accessibility, and dedication to public service. He used his presidency to encourage and inspire millions of Indians, especially students and youth. His presidency was marked by his efforts to make Rashtrapati Bhavan more accessible to common people and his active engagement in educational and developmental initiatives.

DEATH

Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam passed away on July 27, 2015, while delivering a lecture at the Indian Institute of Management Shillong. His legacy as a visionary leader, dedicated scientist, and inspirational figure continues to inspire people across the globe. His life and work exemplify the power of dreams, hard work, and the pursuit of excellence.
